How to Use This Calculator

The Rent Increase Calculator highlights the cumulative financial footprint of renting over a long period. To see your trajectory, input your current monthly rent, your planned annual rent increase percentage, and your estimated annual investment return. The calculator compiles a full 30-year schedule and outlines exactly how much total cash you will hand over to your landlord after 5, 10, 20, and 30 years. It also calculates the opportunity cost of renting by comparing it against a flat home mortgage payment scenario. A beautiful visual Area Chart displays your climbing annual rent alongside your cumulative payments, emphasizing the 'spent with no equity' reality of renting and giving you clear perspective on long-term homeownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does rent increase compound over time? +
Rent increases compound annually, meaning each year's hike is calculated on top of the previous year's rent, leading to rapid price growth.
What is the average rent increase rate per year? +
Historically, rent increases average between 3% and 5% annually, though high-demand metropolitan areas can experience much larger spikes.
Is it better to lock in a mortgage payment? +
Yes! A fixed-rate mortgage locks in your principal and interest payments forever, protecting you from compound rental inflation and building equity.
