What Credit Score Do You Need to Buy a House?

Everything you need to know about the number that dictates your mortgage interest rate.

Minimum Scores by Loan Type

FHA loans are the most flexible, allowing for scores as low as 580 with a 3.5% down payment. Conventional loans typically require a 620. VA loans don't have a hard minimum, but most lenders look for at least a 600.

How Score Affects Your Monthly Payment

A score of 760 might get you a 6.5% interest rate, while a 640 might land you with a 7.5% rate. On a $400,000 home, that 1% difference costs you over $250 every single month for 30 years—that's $90,000 in extra interest.

Fastest Ways to Raise Your Score

1. Pay down credit card balances (utilization is 30% of your score). 2. Dispute errors on your credit report. 3. Become an "authorized user" on a family member's long-standing account. 4. Don't open any new accounts before applying for a mortgage.

Timeline to Improve Your Score

Most credit score improvements take 30–60 days to show up on your report. If your score is low, start working on it at least 6 months before you plan to start house hunting to ensure you get the best possible rates.

Getting Pre-Approved With a Low Score

If your score is below 600, you can still get pre-approved, but you might need a larger down payment (often 10%) or a co-signer. Some specialized lenders focus on "non-QM" loans for those with unique credit situations.
