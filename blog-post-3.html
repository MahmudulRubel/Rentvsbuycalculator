The Hidden Costs of Buying a Home Nobody Talks About

Prepare your bank account for the expenses that go beyond the mortgage payment.

Closing Costs Breakdown (3–6%)

Most buyers save for a down payment but forget that they need an additional 3% to 6% of the purchase price for closing costs. On a $400,000 home, that's up to $24,000 in fees for title insurance, appraisals, and taxes.

Property Taxes and Insurance

These are never static. In many states, property taxes increase every year. If your home value appreciates, so does your tax bill. Homeowners insurance also tends to rise as rebuilding costs increase.

PMI: The Extra Fee You Gain Nothing From

If you put down less than 20%, you'll likely pay Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). This can add $100–$300 to your monthly payment and protects the lender, not you.

Maintenance: The 1% Rule

Experts recommend budgeting 1% of your home's value annually for maintenance. For a $380,000 home, that's $3,800 a year or about $315 a month for things like HVAC service, gutter cleaning, and roof repairs.

Moving and Setup Costs

Moving across town can cost $1,000+, and a cross-country move can be $5,000+. Then there's the cost of new furniture, appliances, and tools (like a lawnmower) that you didn't need as a renter.
