Should You Buy a Condo or a House?

Comparing the lifestyle, finances, and long-term potential of the two most common home types.

Price and HOA Fees

Condos are generally cheaper to purchase than single-family houses in the same neighborhood. However, condos come with monthly Homeowners Association (HOA) fees that can range from $200 to $1,000+. These fees cover shared amenities and exterior maintenance but are a permanent monthly cost.

Maintenance Responsibility

In a house, you are responsible for everything—the roof, the yard, the foundation. In a condo, your maintenance is usually limited to "inside the walls." This makes condos ideal for those who travel often or don't want to spend their weekends mowing a lawn.

Appreciation and Investment Potential

Historically, single-family houses appreciate faster than condos. This is because you own the land, which is the truly scarce resource. Houses also offer more rental flexibility (like AirBnB), whereas condos often have strict rules about subletting.

Financing Differences

Getting a mortgage for a condo can be slightly more difficult. Lenders require the condo association to be "warrantable," meaning they have enough insurance, healthy reserves, and a low percentage of renters. Houses don't have these extra layers of approval.

Who Each Is Right For

A condo is perfect for urban professionals or retirees who want a "lock-and-leave" lifestyle. A house is better for families or those who want complete control over their property and the highest possible long-term appreciation.
