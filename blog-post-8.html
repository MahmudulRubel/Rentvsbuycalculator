How Your City Affects the Rent vs Buy Decision

Real estate is local. See why the "right" decision in Detroit might be the "wrong" one in San Francisco.

Price-to-Rent Ratio Explained

The price-to-rent ratio is calculated by dividing the median home price by the median annual rent. A ratio of 15 or less means buying is likely better. A ratio of 21 or more means renting is probably the smarter financial move.

Cities Where Buying Clearly Wins

In cities like Detroit, Cleveland, and Memphis, home prices are relatively low compared to rents. In these markets, the monthly mortgage payment is often hundreds of dollars less than rent for a similar house, making buying an easy choice.

Cities Where Renting Wins (High-Cost Markets)

In San Francisco, NYC, and Miami, home prices have skyrocketed far beyond what rents can support. In these "rent-heavy" markets, it can take 20+ years for a buyer to reach the breakeven point compared to a renter who invests their savings.

Mid-Tier Cities: The "It Depends" Markets

Cities like Austin, Denver, and Nashville have seen rapid growth. In these markets, the decision often comes down to your "time horizon"—how long you plan to stay. If you're staying for 7+ years, buying often wins; if it's 3 years, renting is safer.

How to Calculate Your Local Ratio

Find a house for sale and a similar house for rent in the same neighborhood. Divide the sale price by 12 months of rent. If the number is high, think twice before buying. If it's low, start looking for a lender.
