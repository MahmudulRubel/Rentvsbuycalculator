First-Time Home Buyer Mistakes That Cost Thousands

Avoid these common pitfalls to ensure your first home purchase is a financial success.

Not Getting Pre-Approved First

Looking at houses before talking to a lender is a recipe for heartbreak. A pre-approval letter tells you exactly what you can afford and shows sellers that you are a serious, qualified buyer.

Skipping the Home Inspection

In a competitive market, you might be tempted to waive the inspection. Don't. A $500 inspection can uncover $50,000 in foundation or roofing issues that you would otherwise inherit.

Buying at the Top of Your Budget

Just because the bank says you qualify for a $500,000 loan doesn't mean you should take it. Leave room in your monthly budget for travel, savings, and the inevitable repairs that come with homeownership.

Ignoring the Neighborhood

You can change the house, but you can't change the location. Visit the neighborhood at different times of the day—especially at night and during rush hour—to get a true feel for the area.

Making Big Purchases Before Closing

Buying a new car or a living room set on credit before your mortgage closes can change your debt-to-income ratio and cause your loan to be denied at the last minute. Wait until you have the keys in hand.

Underestimating Closing Costs

Many buyers forget they need cash for closing costs, which can range from 2% to 5% of the home's price. If you don't have this cash ready, you won't be able to close the deal.
