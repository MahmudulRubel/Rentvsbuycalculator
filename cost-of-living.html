How to Use This Calculator

The City Cost of Living Comparator offers an advanced side-by-side analysis of daily expenses and housing costs between 30 major US metropolitan areas. To compare locations, select your Primary City (e.g. New York, NY) and your Target City (e.g. Austin, TX) from our dropdown selectors. The tool immediately pulls median home values, rents, utility costs, property taxes, grocery rates, and commute profiles for both cities. A stunning 6-axis Radar Chart normalizes these metrics visually, while green indicator highlights pinpoint the winner in each spending category. An overall suitability affordability score makes it simple to plan your relocation budget with absolute clarity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the cost of living index compared? +
Our comparator evaluates 6 core housing and everyday expenses (home prices, rents, food, utilities, taxes, commute) to score relative city affordability.
What factors are included in the cost of living comparison? +
We analyze median home purchase prices, monthly rent averages, property tax rates, monthly utilities, food inflation, and transit commute times.
How does housing cost affect cost of living scores? +
Housing represents the single largest expenditure for households, heavily influencing the overall index and scoring outcomes for regional comparisons.
