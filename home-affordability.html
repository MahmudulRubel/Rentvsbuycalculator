How to Use This Calculator

The Home Affordability Calculator evaluates your maximum safe purchase price by analyzing your financial profiles against commercial underwriting guidelines. To find your budget, enter your pre-tax annual household gross income and your recurring monthly debt liabilities, such as auto loans, student loans, or credit cards. Input your target interest rate, down payment cash, property tax rate, homeowners insurance premium, and monthly HOA fees. Our calculator uses the standard 28/36 debt-to-income (DTI) rules to outline three scenarios: Conservative, Moderate, and Aggressive affordability. A dynamic horizontal bar chart displays your affordable home price alongside an active gauge indicating whether your proposed monthly housing expenses fall into the safety zone or enter the warning threshold, giving you total confidence in your purchasing decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the home affordability calculator work? +
Our calculator evaluates your gross monthly income and existing debts against standard lending debt-to-income (DTI) rules to estimate a safe target home price.
What is the 28/36 debt-to-income rule? +
The 28/36 rule states that your housing costs should not exceed 28% of your gross income, and your total monthly debt payments (housing + debt) should not exceed 36%.
How does a down payment affect my budget? +
A larger down payment directly increases your affordable home price because it reduces the mortgage size you need, keeping your monthly payments within safe limits.
