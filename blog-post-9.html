What Is Home Equity and How Do You Build It Faster?

Understand the engine of real estate wealth and how to accelerate your financial growth.

Equity Defined Simply

Home equity is the difference between what your home is worth and what you owe on your mortgage. If your house is worth $400,000 and you owe $250,000, you have $150,000 in equity. It is effectively the portion of the home that you "own."

How Equity Builds Over Time

Equity builds in two ways: paydown and appreciation. Every month, a portion of your mortgage payment goes toward the principal balance (paydown). Meanwhile, as the market value of your home increases, your equity grows (appreciation).

LTV Ratio: Why It Matters

The Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is your mortgage balance divided by the home's value. Lenders use this to determine your risk. Once your LTV drops below 80%, you can usually remove Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), saving you money instantly.

6 Strategies to Build Equity Faster

1. Make extra principal payments. 2. Choose a 15-year mortgage instead of a 30-year. 3. Make smart home improvements (kitchens and bathrooms). 4. Avoid cash-out refinances. 5. Round up your monthly payment. 6. Pay bi-weekly instead of monthly.

Equity as a Retirement Tool

For many Americans, home equity is their largest asset in retirement. A paid-off home eliminates your biggest monthly expense, allowing your retirement savings to last much longer.
