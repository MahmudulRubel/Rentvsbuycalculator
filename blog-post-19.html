How to Read a Mortgage Statement

Master your monthly statement to ensure every dollar is going where it should.

Principal, Interest, and Escrow

Your monthly payment is typically split three ways. Principal reduces your loan balance. Interest is the cost of borrowing. Escrow is a "holding tank" for your property taxes and homeowners insurance. The lender pays these bills on your behalf once a year.

Understanding the Escrow Analysis

Once a year, your lender will perform an "escrow analysis." If your property taxes went up, your monthly payment will increase to cover the shortfall. If they went down, you might get a refund check. This is why your "fixed" mortgage payment can still change.

What Is a Suspense Account?

If you make a partial payment, the lender often puts the money into a "suspense account" instead of applying it to your loan. They will hold it there until you pay the remaining balance. Always make full payments to avoid this.

Spotting Errors Early

Check your principal balance every month. Ensure that any extra payments you made were correctly applied to the "Principal" and not to "Next month's interest." Misapplied payments can cost you thousands in the long run.

PMI Removal Triggers

Look for your "current LTV" or principal balance. Once you owe less than 80% of your home's original value, you have the legal right to request the removal of Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI).
