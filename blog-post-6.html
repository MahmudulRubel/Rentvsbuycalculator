Fixed vs Adjustable Rate Mortgage

Choosing the right loan structure can save you thousands over the life of your mortgage.

How Fixed-Rate Mortgages Work

A fixed-rate mortgage keeps the same interest rate for the entire life of the loan (usually 15 or 30 years). Your principal and interest payment will never change, offering maximum stability and protection against rising interest rates.

How ARMs Work (5/1, 7/1 Explained)

An Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM) offers a lower interest rate for an initial "fixed" period (like 5 or 7 years). After that, the rate adjusts annually based on market conditions. A 5/1 ARM means the rate is fixed for 5 years and then adjusts every 1 year.

When ARMs Make Sense

ARMs are smart if you know you will sell or refinance the home before the fixed period ends. If you are a medical resident moving for a 4-year residency, a 5/1 ARM could save you hundreds of dollars a month compared to a 30-year fixed loan.

When Fixed Wins

If this is your "forever home" or if interest rates are currently at historic lows, a fixed-rate mortgage is the clear winner. It eliminates the risk of your payment ballooning in 10 years if inflation causes rates to skyrocket.

Refinancing as a Strategy

Many buyers choose an ARM with the plan to refinance into a fixed-rate loan if rates drop in the future. This is a common strategy, but it requires that you have enough equity and a good credit score when it's time to refinance.
