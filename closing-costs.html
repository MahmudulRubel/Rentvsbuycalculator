How to Use This Calculator

The Closing Costs Estimator helps you plan for the transactional fees paid on the day of closing. To get started, select your US state from our dropdown menu to auto-configure regional averages, and input your target home price. Enter your planned down payment, mortgage rate, and choose your loan program type (Conventional, FHA, or VA). You can also toggle between a Low and High estimate range depending on local quote variations. The calculator instantly projects your total closing costs, displaying an itemized fee breakdown table mapping lender fees, third-party fees (like appraisal and title services), government transfer taxes, and prepaid escrows (taxes and insurance). A horizontal bar chart visualizes these segments side-by-side, preparing you fully for home purchasing day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are closing costs when buying a house? +
Closing costs are transaction fees paid at the end of a home purchase, covering lender fees, title insurance, government taxes, and prepaid escrows.
How much are closing costs on average? +
Closing costs typically average between 2% and 5% of the total home purchase price, varying based on location and loan program type.
How do FHA and VA closing costs differ? +
FHA loans require upfront mortgage insurance premiums, while VA loans feature a one-time funding fee instead of recurring private mortgage insurance.
