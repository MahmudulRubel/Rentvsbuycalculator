How to Use This Calculator

The Home Equity Calculator evaluates the total value stored in your property and estimates your available borrowing power. To use this calculator, enter the current estimated market value of your home, followed by your remaining mortgage balance. If you are interested in a Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) or home equity loan, you can customize the typical 80% combined loan-to-value (CLTV) borrowing limit. The calculator instantly displays your total home equity, your active Loan-to-Value (LTV) percentage on a visual gauge, and your maximum eligible HELOC line. The results are illustrated via a stacked bar chart mapping your remaining principal debt, your accessible equity, and your inaccessible buffer, giving you full control over your borrowing capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is home equity calculated? +
Home equity is calculated by subtracting your current outstanding mortgage balance from the current market value of your property.
What is a good Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio? +
LTV represents your mortgage balance as a percentage of home value. An LTV below 80% is ideal, as it unlocks low refinance rates and avoids PMI.
How much equity can I borrow against via HELOC? +
Most lenders allow you to borrow up to a combined loan-to-value (CLTV) limit of 80% to 85% of your home's appraised value, minus current mortgage balance.
