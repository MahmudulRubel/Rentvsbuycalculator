How to Buy a Home With Low Income

Homeownership is more accessible than you think, even if your paycheck is modest.

FHA and USDA Loans

FHA loans allow for a 3.5% down payment and are flexible with credit scores. USDA loans offer 0% down for homes in qualified rural and suburban areas. These programs are specifically designed for low-to-moderate income buyers.

State-Level Down Payment Assistance (DPA)

Every state has a Housing Finance Agency (HFA) that offers grants or second mortgages to cover your down payment. Some programs are "forgivable," meaning you don't have to pay the money back if you stay in the home for a set number of years.

HUD Programs and Good Neighbor Next Door

The Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) offers several programs. "Good Neighbor Next Door" provides a 50% discount on homes in certain areas for teachers, firefighters, and police officers.

Section 8 Homeownership Vouchers

Few people realize that Section 8 housing vouchers can actually be used to pay a mortgage, not just rent. This program helps very low-income families build equity instead of paying a landlord.

Co-Borrower Strategies

If your income alone isn't enough, consider a co-borrower (like a parent or sibling). Their income can help you qualify for a larger loan, provided they are willing to share the legal responsibility for the mortgage.
