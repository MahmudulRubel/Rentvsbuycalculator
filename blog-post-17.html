How Real Estate Agents Get Paid

Understand the 2024 changes to commission structures and what it means for your wallet.

The Traditional 5–6% Commission

Historically, the seller paid a 5–6% commission, which was split between the seller's agent and the buyer's agent. As a buyer, this often felt "free," but in reality, the cost was baked into the home's sale price.

The 2024 NAR Settlement Changes

Following a major legal settlement in 2024, the way buyer's agents are paid has changed. Sellers are no longer required to offer a set commission to buyer's agents in the MLS. This means buyers might now be responsible for paying their own agent's fee directly, or negotiating for the seller to cover it.

Negotiating Agent Fees

Because of these changes, agent fees are more negotiable than ever. You can negotiate for a flat fee, an hourly rate, or a lower percentage. Always discuss compensation upfront before signing a buyer representation agreement.

When to Use an Agent vs Go Alone

An agent provides expertise in pricing, negotiation, and paperwork. If you are a first-time buyer, having an expert can save you from making a $10,000 mistake. However, if you are an experienced investor, you might choose to go alone to save on the commission.

Questions to Ask Your Agent

"What is your fee structure?", "How will you be compensated if the seller isn't offering a commission?", and "Can I cancel our agreement if I'm not satisfied?" These questions ensure transparency and protect your interests during the purchase.
