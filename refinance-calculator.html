How to Use This Calculator

The Mortgage Refinance Calculator analyzes whether replacing your existing mortgage loan with a new one makes financial sense. To start, input your current remaining loan balance, your current interest rate, and the remaining term of your loan. In the next section, input your proposed new interest rate, the new term (e.g. 15 or 30 years), and the estimated refinancing closing costs. The calculator instantly compares your old and new loan amortization schedules, calculating your monthly payment savings, total lifetime interest savings, and the exact break-even point in months. A visual line chart outlines your cumulative refinance costs versus cumulative savings, making it simple to see precisely when your transaction switches from a cost into a massive financial gain.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is refinancing a mortgage worth it? +
Refinancing is typically worth it if you can lower your interest rate by 0.75% or more, or if you plan to stay in the home long enough to recover closing costs.
How is the refinance break-even point calculated? +
The break-even point is found by dividing the total refinance closing costs by your monthly payment savings. This gives the exact number of months to recoup costs.
What are typical refinance closing costs? +
Refinance closing costs typically range between 2% and 5% of the new loan amount, which covers application, appraisal, title, and origination fees.
