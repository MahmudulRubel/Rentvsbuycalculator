The True Cost of Renting for 10 Years vs Buying

A decade-long analysis of where your money actually goes.

Cumulative Rent vs Cumulative Mortgage

If you rent for $2,200/month with a 3% annual increase, you will spend over $300,000 on rent in 10 years. In contrast, a mortgage payment stays fixed (mostly). While you pay interest, a significant portion of your money stays with you as equity.

Equity: The Wealth Engine

After 10 years, a buyer who bought a $400,000 home might have $80,000 in principal paid down and $100,000 in appreciation gains. That's $180,000 in wealth that a renter simply doesn't have.

The Opportunity Cost of a Down Payment

The buyer's biggest "cost" is the $80,000 down payment. If the renter had invested that $80,000 in the stock market at 8%, they would have $172,000 after 10 years. This is the main reason why renting sometimes wins—the power of compounding investments.

Net Worth Comparison at Year 10

In most markets, the buyer's net worth (equity) will exceed the renter's net worth (invested down payment) between years 5 and 7. After year 7, the buyer pulls ahead rapidly as their housing costs remain stable while the renter's rent continues to climb.

Conclusion: It Depends on 3 Variables

The winner depends on: 1. Home appreciation rate. 2. Stock market return rate. 3. How long you stay. Our calculator uses these exact variables to give you the most accurate answer for your specific situation.
