How to Save a Down Payment in 2 Years

A realistic, month-by-month roadmap to reaching your homeownership goals.

How Much Do You Actually Need?

You don't always need 20%. FHA loans allow for 3.5% down, and some conventional loans only require 3%. On a $300,000 home, that's $10,500. Add $10,000 for closing costs, and your goal is $20,500—much more achievable than $60,000.

Automating Your Success

If you don't see the money, you won't spend it. Set up a direct deposit from your paycheck into a separate High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA). Even $500 a month adds up to $12,000 over two years before interest.

Cutting the Biggest Expenses

To reach a big goal fast, you need big cuts. Consider "house hacking" your current rental by getting a roommate for two years, or skipping vacations in exchange for a weekend "staycation." These big wins move the needle faster than skipping lattes.

Side Income Strategies

Many successful savers pick up a side hustle dedicated solely to the down payment. Driving for Uber, freelancing, or selling unused items can generate an extra $500–$1,000 a month that goes straight into the house fund.

Down Payment Assistance Programs

Most states have programs for first-time buyers that offer grants or low-interest loans for down payments. Some programs can provide up to $20,000 if you meet income requirements. Always check with a local housing counselor.
